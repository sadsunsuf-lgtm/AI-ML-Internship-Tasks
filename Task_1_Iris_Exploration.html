Visualizing Relationships

Scatter Plot

In [6]:
# Setting the aesthetic style of the plots
sns.set_theme(style="whitegrid")

plt.figure(figsize=(10, 6))
sns.scatterplot(data=df, x='sepal_length', y='sepal_width', hue='species', palette='viridis', s=70)

plt.title('Sepal Length vs Sepal Width (Grouped by Species)', fontsize=15)
plt.xlabel('Sepal Length (cm)')
plt.ylabel('Sepal Width (cm)')
plt.legend(title='Iris Species')
plt.show()

Observation: The scatter plot reveals a clear cluster for the Setosa species (purple/darker dots), showing it has smaller sepal length but larger sepal width compared to the others. Versicolor and Virginica have some overlap, suggesting they are more closely related in sepal dimensions.
